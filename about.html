Hello, my name is

Jack T. Drabenstadt

I'm a double major in Computer Science and Digital Narrative and Interactive Design with a minor in Information Science at the University of Pittsburgh, combining technical skills in coding and software development with storytelling and interactive design. My studies allow me to explore on how digital media, such as games, shape our experiences and influence society, culture, and human behavior.

As a Student Technical Consultant at the University, I assist students and faculty with technical troubleshooting and support. Additionally, I work as a Barista, where I've honed my customer service, multitasking, and time-management skills. I will also be conducting research at Pitt's Resilient Systems and Societies Lab under Dr. Amy Babay, where I will focus on modeling critical infrastructure resilience against compound threats, such as natural disasters and cyberattacks, and designing adaptive system architectures to improve recovery and adaptability.

I am passionate about the intersection of technology and creativity, particularly in fields like game development, interactive art, and creative coding. I enjoy using my technical expertise to craft engaging, story-driven experiences and experiment with innovative forms of interactive media.

In addition to my work in interactive design and storytelling, I have a strong interest in computer networking. I am particularly fascinated by how systems communicate and interact, as well as the design and implementation of network architectures that support efficiency, scalability, and resilience. These distinct areas of interest reflect my curiosity for both the creative and technical sides of computing.
